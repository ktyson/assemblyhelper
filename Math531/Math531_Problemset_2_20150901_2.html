Question: A wall (side a of a right triangle) is 10 units tall. A pole leaning against the wall is the c side (hypotenuse). Moving the pole out 1 unit will make it the b side to a new hypotenuse. How long is the pole (begins as c side and ends as b side)?

Answer: 50 units. By method of repeated trials seeking a difference of 1 unit between sides b and c: the pole on side c (hypotenuse) that starts at 50 units with side b at 49 units, then one withdraws 1 unit, by pulling the pole out, to equal 49 units, which collapses to become the new side b.
